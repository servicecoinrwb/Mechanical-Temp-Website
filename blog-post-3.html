Proactive maintenance tips for facility managers in Metro Detroit.

Why Early Maintenance is Crucial

For facility managers in Southfield and across Metro Detroit, the first major snowfall isn't the time to discover a problem with your heating system. A Michigan winter places extreme demand on commercial HVAC units. Proactive maintenance in the fall prevents emergency breakdowns, ensures tenant comfort, and controls operational costs.

Your Fall Commercial HVAC Checklist

A comprehensive system check-up should be a priority. Here are three key areas our technicians focus on before winter hits:

1. Inspect and Test the Heat Exchanger

The heat exchanger is the heart of your furnace. Even a small crack can leak carbon monoxide, posing a serious health risk. A thorough inspection is non-negotiable for ensuring the safety of your building's occupants.

2. Calibrate Thermostats and Controls

Inaccurate thermostat readings can lead to inefficient system performance and uncomfortable temperature fluctuations. Calibrating all controls and sensors ensures your system operates as intended, saving energy and preventing unnecessary wear and tear.

3. Clean and Replace Filters

This is one of the simplest yet most effective maintenance tasks. Clogged filters restrict airflow, forcing the system to work harder. Replacing them improves air quality and system efficiency, leading to lower energy bills.

Schedule Your Winter Prep Today

Don't wait for a system failure during the first deep freeze. A proactive approach guarantees a safe, warm, and efficient winter for your commercial property.
