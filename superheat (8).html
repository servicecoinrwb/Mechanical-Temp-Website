📖 READING THE GAUGES

Two numbers tell the story of a cooling system. Get both and you can name almost any fault.

SUPERHEAT
How much the refrigerant gas warmed up past boiling on the cold (suction) side. Take it as:

suction line temp − suction saturation temp

SUBCOOLING
How much the liquid cooled below condensing on the hot (liquid) side. Take it as:

head saturation temp − liquid line temp

Your gauges give you the saturation temps; a clamp thermometer gives the line temps. Healthy is roughly 8–14° for each.

When two readings look alike, the other one breaks the tie: low charge and a restriction both run high superheat — subcooling (low vs high) tells them apart.

You don't have to memorize this. ROOKIE mode does the subtraction and flags what's out of range. PRO mode hands you the raw numbers and lets you call it. Either way — match the pattern, make the call.
